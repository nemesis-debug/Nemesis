Types of Information

1. Primary Information – Firsthand or original data, such as research studies, interviews, surveys, and eyewitness reports.

2. Secondary Information – Interpretation or analysis of primary sources, such as textbooks, reviews, and documentaries.

3. Tertiary Information – A summary or compilation of primary and secondary sources, like encyclopedias, indexes, and databases.

Types of Media Sources

1. Traditional Media – Print (newspapers, magazines), broadcast (radio, TV), and outdoor (billboards, posters).

2. Digital Media – Websites, social media, blogs, and online articles.

3. Multimedia – A combination of text, images, audio, video, and interactive content (e.g., YouTube videos, podcasts).

How to Evaluate Media and Information Sources

1. Accuracy – Check if the information is supported by evidence and sources.

2. Credibility – Consider the author's expertise, reputation, and affiliations.

3. Objectivity – Look for bias and whether the source presents balanced viewpoints.

4. Currency – Ensure the information is up-to-date and relevant.

5. Relevance – Determine if the content fits your needs and purpose.
